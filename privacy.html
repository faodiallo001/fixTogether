Privacy & Data

Your words are sensitive. This page explains what we collect, how we use it, and how we protect it. By using FixTogether, you agree to this Privacy Notice. This text is for information only and does not replace local privacy laws that may apply to you.

1. What we collect

When you use FixTogether, we may collect:

  • Conflict text: the words you write when you describe what happened and how you feel.
  • Responses: the words written by the other person when they use your Fix link.
  • Contact details: your email address or the email address you provide so we can send the Fix or a copy to you.
  • Technical data: basic logs such as IP address, browser type, device information and time of access, mainly for security, abuse-prevention and analytics.

2. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • read both sides of the conflict and generate your Fix,
  • send the Fix or related emails that you request,
  • operate, maintain and protect the site,
  • detect misuse or abuse of the service, and
  • create anonymised or aggregated statistics that do not identify you, to help improve FixTogether.

We do not sell your personal information to third-party marketers.

3. How long we keep your words

FixTogether is designed as a calm, temporary space — not a permanent archive of your conflict.

We keep the text of your Fix in our active systems only for as long as reasonably needed to:

  • generate and deliver your Fix,
  • ensure the service is working correctly, and
  • protect the service from abuse, fraud or technical issues.

After that, your words are removed from our active systems. Backup copies or logs may remain for a limited time for security, technical or legal reasons, but are not used to create new Fixes.

4. Sharing your information

We may share your information only in these situations:

  • Service providers: with trusted processors that help us run FixTogether (for example, cloud hosting, email delivery or AI text processing), under contracts that require them to protect your data and use it only for our service.
  • When you choose to share: if you send a Fix link to another person, they will see the information you decide to include in that Fix.
  • Legal reasons: if we are required to do so by law, regulation or a valid legal request, or if needed to protect our rights, users or the public.

We do not contact the other person for you. You stay in control of who receives the Fix.

5. Cookies and analytics

FixTogether may use cookies or similar technologies to remember your settings, keep the site secure and understand how the service is used (for example, which pages are visited most). Any analytics we use are focused on improving the product, not building advertising profiles.

6. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data against unauthorised access, loss or misuse. No online service can be 100% secure, but we design FixTogether with privacy and discretion in mind.

You can help by not including passwords, bank details or government ID numbers in your Fix, and by sharing your Fix link only with people you trust.

7. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal data, such as the right to access, correct or delete information, or to object to certain types of processing.

If you want to exercise these rights or have questions about your data, you can contact us at the email below. We may need to verify your identity before answering your request.

8. International use

FixTogether may be used by people in different countries. Your data may be processed in a country that is different from yours, where data-protection laws may be different. We take steps to handle your data in a way that is consistent with this Privacy Notice.

9. Changes to this notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. When we do, we will change the date at the bottom of this page. If the changes are important, we may highlight them on the site. If you continue to use FixTogether after an update, it means you accept the new version.
